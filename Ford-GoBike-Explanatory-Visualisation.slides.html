Ford BikeGo Explanatory Visualisation¶

by Anita Obarakpor¶

Ford GoBike is a bike sharing system available in San Fransisco. Similar to other bike share systems, Ford GoBike consists of a fleet of bikes that can be unlocked in one station and returned in any other network station. Thus, this is ideal for one-way trips. These bikes are available for use thorughout the year and riders have access to all bikes in the network.

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I want to look at what factors contribute to a greater frequency of rides. The variables i will focus on are gender, user type, age and the time and date of rides.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset has 3023992 bike share trips that took place within the San Francisco Bay Area. The attributes recorded are

  • bike id, start and end station id as strings
  • start and end station latitude and longitudes as floats
  • user status(subscriber or just a customer)
  • gender of the user
  • trip start time and duration
  • income level of the trip
  • the specific time in terms of date, hour, day of the week , month and year, the trips were taken
  • estimated distance travelled on rides
  • age group of riders

User by Gender and Age Group¶

Checking the number of trips taken by both genders and the different age group, almost three-quarters of rides were taken by male compared to females. Approximately 73% of rides were taken by users between 20 and 40 years of age which means the rides are most popular among makes and young adults.

Subscribed or Not?¶

Looking at the count of trips taken by both user types, it is easy to see that most rides (almost 90%) are taken by people with an active subscription

Has the service grown over time?¶

Generally, there has been a upward trend in the number of rides taken since the inception in June of 2017 which means that the business has seen some growth.

When are the most popular times for rides?¶

Among, subscribers,the hours in which rides are most frequent correspond with those of a typical work/school commute (7-9am and 4-6pm) while for customers without a subscription rides are mostly popular on weekends for leisure.

Relationship Between Age/User Type and Trip Distance/Age¶

There is no linear correlation between age or and length of trips. However, there is a subtle parabolic curve that shows that riders between 20 and 40 years take the most and the longest trips in terms of distance. The regression shows that females and non_subscribed customers take slighlty longer trips on average than males and subscribers. This could mean that the average work/school commute is a relatively short trip.
